===================================================================
BRAND VELOCITY INDEX (BVI) - SCORING SPECIFICATION v2.1
Power Digital Marketing - Confidential
Supersedes v1.2 and v2.0. Baseline-relative, season-neutral scoring.
===================================================================

This document governs all BVI score calculations. Any deviation from
this spec in code or tooling is a bug, not a feature. Changes require a
version increment and internal sign-off.

v2.0 replaced month-over-month (MoM) scoring with baseline-relative
scoring. v2.1 fixes a seasonality flaw in v2.0: it moves the baseline
onto the season-neutral full-cycle mean (2.5) and adds a seasonal-
adjustment layer for the volume signals (0.6, 3.2). The question the BVI
answers is "how far is the brand above or below its own starting point,
after removing the part of the movement the category and the season
explain." Inputs, metrics, and CSV structure are unchanged. Only the
scoring lens changes. Every element of v1.2 is carried forward.

STATE OF THIS SPEC: implementation-ready. One input is pending - the
per-month seasonal coefficients for the volume signals, which come from a
pooled fashion-client data pull (see Section 13). The engine is built to
run correctly without them (seasonal layer defaults to a no-op) and to
improve when they are loaded, with no logic change. Everything else is
final.



-------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 0: DECISIONS OF RECORD
-------------------------------------------------------------------
The following were open decisions during design. All are now settled and
implemented. Each records the alternative that was considered and rejected,
so the reasoning is auditable. Nothing in this section is outstanding.

0.1 BASELINE = SEASON-NEUTRAL FULL-CYCLE MEAN. SETTLED (revised from v2.0).
    The reference anchor is the mean of the client's first full cycle of
    backfill (up to 12 months), locked once a full cycle exists. Every month
    with data is scored as a deviation from that reference; no month is
    excluded.
    Chosen over v2.0's "first 3 months" because a 3-month anchor sits inside
    one season: if those months are a Q4 peak the whole series reads as
    decline, and if they are a Q1 trough it reads as inflated. A full-cycle
    mean contains one of every month, so the reference itself is season-
    neutral and cannot be a Q4 or Q1 anchor. It also uses all backfill rather
    than a quarter of it (steadier, less anomaly-prone) and scores all 12
    backfill months on day one rather than 9.
    Rejected: fixed first-N-months (seasonal anchor bias, the flaw above);
    expanding baseline (score moves because the reference moved).
    Immutability: a metric with a full cycle locks its baseline and never
    recomputes. A metric with less than a full cycle (typically social at
    onboarding, 90 days) cannot have a season-neutral reference yet; its
    baseline is provisional and expands until it reaches a full cycle, then
    locks. Flagged PROVISIONAL_BASELINE until then (6.1). See 2.5.
    IMPORTANT INTERIM LIMIT: a season-neutral baseline removes ANCHOR bias
    immediately, but a scored month still carries its own seasonal component
    (a November still reads high for being November) until the seasonal-
    adjustment layer is populated (0.6). See 3.2 and Section 13.

0.2 CATEGORY ENTERS IN TWO DISTINCT PLACES. SETTLED.
    (a) As the deseasonalizing reference applied to ONE signal only:
        Search Demand's brand Trends index (4.1 signal C).
    (b) As its own scored dimension, Category Context, redefined to
        measure share-of-category-attention and brand-vs-category gap
        (4.5) - relative quantities that can move independently of the
        deseasonalized growth in (a).
    Chosen to keep category prominent (its own dimension) while also
    removing category seasonality from search interest. The two uses carry
    distinct math, so they are not the same measurement.
    Rejected: category as deseasonalizer only (would gut the dimension);
    category in the dimension only (would leave search seasonal).
    Residual correlation between (a) and (b) is a post-launch validation
    item, not a blocker (Section 11.3). See 4.1 and 4.5.

0.3 RISING TIDE NO LONGER APPLIES A 0.90 MULTIPLIER. SETTLED.
    In v1.2 rising tide multiplied the Search Trends score by 0.90.
    Under v2.0 the category is subtracted from the Search Trends signal
    continuously (3.2, 4.1), which is the precise form of the same
    correction. A multiplier on top would correct for the category twice
    and over-penalize a brand growing while its category is also healthy.
    Rising tide is retained as a displayed flag and as a badge modifier
    (a category-driven gain reads as Watch, not Improving), but not as a
    score multiplier. This is the one behavioral change from v1.2 and is
    deliberate. See 4.5, 6.4.

0.4 SMALL-BASE THRESHOLDS. SETTLED as committed launch values.
    The floors that decide when a tiny baseline value is scored as a ratio,
    scored in point-mode, or held display-only (4.6) are set to committed
    launch values. They carry a documented retune trigger (any real client
    that trips SMALL_BASE), not an open decision. Low risk for established
    fashion brands. See 4.6 and 3.6.

0.5 CALIBRATION CONSTANTS. SETTLED as committed launch calibration.
    The sensitivities that convert movement into BVI points (Section 3) are
    committed launch values, set by a coherence pass on the mapping (3.6):
    a brand growing a broad ~15% above baseline, net of category, lands
    near BVI 63; ~30% near the low 70s; exceptional growth reaches the low
    80s; decline is symmetric. Score direction and ordering are correct at
    these values; absolute levels are the launch calibration and refine
    against real client history via the retune trigger in 3.6. This is the
    finished state achievable before client data exists; it is not an open
    decision.

0.6 SEASONAL-ADJUSTMENT LAYER. SETTLED design; coefficients PENDING data.
    A season-neutral baseline (0.1) removes anchor bias but not each month's
    own seasonal component. The volume signals (branded impressions, branded
    clicks, direct sessions, organic sessions, organic reach, follower growth)
    have no category comparator and so cannot be deseasonalized the way search
    interest is. The fix is a seasonal-index table that records, per fashion
    sub-vertical and calendar month, how far each volume family typically runs
    above or below its annual mean; the engine subtracts that expected
    seasonal level so only movement BEYOND the seasonal norm scores (3.2).
    Why the coefficients are pending: a seasonal shape cannot be estimated
    from one client's single year (12 points cannot separate "November is
    always high" from "the brand grew"). It must be pooled across many fashion
    clients. That is the data pull described in Section 13.
    Engine state: the layer is fully built. The table (SEASONAL_INDEX) is
    empty at launch and is a documented no-op when empty, so the engine runs
    correctly today and improves when the table is loaded, with no logic
    change. See 3.2, Section 13.


-------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 1: CORE PRINCIPLES
-------------------------------------------------------------------

1.1 RELATIVE SCORING - NON-NEGOTIABLE (carried from v1.2, re-based)
    All scoring is relative, never absolute. v1.2 scored relative to the
    prior month. v2.0 scores relative to a fixed baseline and, for search
    interest, relative to the category. Size-agnosticism is preserved:
    every ratio metric is scored as a log-ratio to its own baseline, so a
    brand with 10K branded impressions and one with 2M are compared on the
    same growth scale. Absolute values are context. Position vs baseline,
    net of category, is health.

1.2 DETERMINISTIC CALCULATION (carried, reaffirmed)
    Given the same input files and the same constants in Section 3, the
    BVI is identical on every run, every model version, every date. The
    baseline is defined by the client's earliest months, which are
    immutable (history is append-only), so appending later months never
    changes it. The only estimated quantity is per-signal volatility
    (6.3), which is a deterministic standard deviation and feeds ONLY the
    uncertainty band and the badge - never the headline number. Claude API
    is called once per month for narrative only (Section 7). Claude does
    not calculate, override, or influence any score.

1.3 DATA HONESTY (carried, unchanged)
    Missing data is excluded, never estimated, filled, or approximated.
    An excluded signal drops out and the remaining weights renormalize.
    Every exclusion is flagged visibly.

1.4 SIGNAL COMPOSITION INTEGRITY (carried, re-based)
    A BVI is comparable across months when the same dimensions are active.
    Because all scored months share one fixed baseline, they are directly
    comparable by construction. When the active set changes (for example
    social maturing into the score), the month is flagged SIGNAL_CHANGE
    and the MoM delta is withheld. A score should move because the brand
    moved, not because coverage changed.

1.5 REALISTIC SCORE RANGE (carried, re-based)
    50 is neutral: the brand sits at its baseline, net of category.
    A healthy brand growing beyond its baseline scores in the 45-85 band.
    Below 45 means sustained decline relative to baseline across
    dimensions. Above 85 is rare and requires strong brand-specific growth
    across dimensions. The range emerges from the Section 3 sensitivities
    and the per-signal cap; it is not hard-clamped at 85.

1.6 TWO-LAYER DESIGN (new; see Section 10)
    The engine computes in log-growth and percentage-point space and
    deseasonalizes against the category. The surface (dashboard, narrative)
    restates that in plain language. The mapping between the layers is the
    fixed set of constants in Section 3 and is fully documented here so
    anyone may dig into it.

SECTION 2: DIMENSIONS, WEIGHTS, DATA SOURCES, BASELINE
-------------------------------------------------------------------

2.1 BRONZE TIER (base - always active if data available)
    Dimension              Weight    Source
    ---------------------------------------------------------------
    Search Demand          22%       GSC (ZIP) + Google Trends Export 1
    Digital Presence       22%       GA4 Traffic Acquisition CSV
    Organic Social         14%       Instagram + TikTok (manual entry)
    Competitive Position   14%       Google Trends Export 1
    Category Context        8%       Google Trends Export 2 (+ SEMrush display)
    ---------------------------------------------------------------
    Bronze subtotal        80%

2.2 SILVER TIER (adds to Bronze - future build)
    Social Listening       10%       Meltwater
    Paid Brand Efficiency   5%       Google Ads + Meta Ads

2.3 GOLD TIER (adds to Silver - future build)
    Direct Awareness        5%       PDM Survey (Pollfish)

2.4 WEIGHT NORMALIZATION (carried, unchanged in principle)
    Weights assume all dimensions in the tier are active. Any dimension
    excluded for missing data or an unfinished baseline drops out, and the
    remaining active-dimension weights renormalize to sum to 100%.
    The same renormalization applies WITHIN a dimension across its signals:
    if a signal is excluded (missing, or small-base display-only), the
    remaining signals in that dimension renormalize.

2.5 BASELINE DEFINITION (season-neutral full-cycle mean; see 0.1)
    For each metric, the baseline is the arithmetic mean of the metric's
    first full cycle of data, BASELINE_CYCLE months (default 12). Because a
    full year contains every season, the reference is season-neutral. Every
    month that carries data is scored as a deviation from this reference;
    no month is excluded from scoring.
    Locking: once BASELINE_CYCLE months exist for a metric, its baseline
    locks and never recomputes (determinism, 1.2). A metric with fewer than
    BASELINE_CYCLE months cannot be season-neutral yet; its baseline is the
    mean of what exists and expands with each new month until a full cycle is
    reached, then locks. Flagged PROVISIONAL_BASELINE until locked (6.1).

    Consequences, stated plainly:
    - GSC / Trends / GA4 (12 months at onboarding): baseline locks at
      onboarding and all 12 months score on day one. No baseline-period
      exclusion, no method seam.
    - Organic Social (90 days at onboarding): baseline is the 3-month mean,
      provisional, and expands monthly until it reaches a full cycle (about
      nine months of live tracking later), then locks. Social scores from its
      first month but carries PROVISIONAL_BASELINE and a wider uncertainty
      band until locked, because a sub-cycle baseline is not season-neutral.
      The seasonal-adjustment layer (0.6) reduces this gap once loaded.
    - The interim seasonality limit from 0.1 applies to every metric until
      the seasonal-adjustment layer is loaded: the season-neutral baseline
      removes anchor bias, but a scored month still carries its own seasonal
      component. Read trajectory over a full cycle, not single months, in the
      interim (3.2, Section 13).

SECTION 3: MOVEMENT-TO-SCORE MAPPING (replaces v1.2 Section 3 scales)
-------------------------------------------------------------------
v1.2 used lookup tables that mapped a MoM change to a score, then averaged
those scores. v2.0 removes the tables for two reasons: they assumed every
metric has the same natural step size (false), and averaging the outputs of
a nonlinear table has no clean interpretation. v2.0 measures each signal's
movement against its baseline, converts movement to BVI points with a
single linear sensitivity, assembles the composite, and applies the range
once. This is the engine layer of the two-layer design (1.6, Section 10).

3.1 MOVEMENT, BY METRIC FAMILY
    Ratio metrics (impressions, clicks, Trends index, sessions, reach,
      follower growth): movement = ln(value / baseline). Zero means at
      baseline. This is scale-free, so it is comparable across brands and
      across the two Trends queries despite their different normalization.
    Percentage-point metrics (direct % of sessions, engagement rate,
      brand share, gap, category share): movement = value - baseline, in
      points.
    Position (GSC average position, lower is better): movement =
      baseline_position - current_position, so improvement is positive.

3.2 DESEASONALIZATION - TWO MECHANISMS
    (a) CATEGORY SUBTRACTION (search interest; see 0.2). For the Search
        Demand brand Trends index signal only, subtract the category's own
        baseline-relative movement:
            brand_specific = ln(brand/brand_base) - ln(category/category_base)
        This removes the shared category component continuously and is the
        generalization of v1.2 rising tide from a binary flag into the method.
        Applied to exactly one signal to avoid double counting with Category
        Context (4.5).
    (b) SEASONAL-INDEX SUBTRACTION (volume signals; see 0.6). The volume
        signals have no category comparator, so each has its calendar-month
        seasonal level subtracted in log space:
            brand_specific = ln(value/baseline) - ln(seasonal_index[m][family])
        seasonal_index is the typical level of that family in that calendar
        month relative to its annual mean, by fashion sub-vertical.
        Signals adjusted: branded impressions, branded clicks, direct
        sessions, organic sessions, organic reach, follower growth rate.
        NOT adjusted here: the search Trends index (uses mechanism (a)
        instead - never both), and the point/share/gap/rate signals (direct
        %, engagement rate, brand share, gap, category share), which are
        near season-neutral by construction.
        PENDING DATA: seasonal_index is empty at launch and the subtraction
        is a no-op until it is populated from the pooled fashion pull. When
        empty, a scored month carries its own seasonal component; read
        trajectory over a full cycle, not single months (Section 13). No code
        change is needed to activate it - only the table and, optionally, a
        calibration retune (3.6).

3.3 SIGNAL CONTRIBUTION
    contribution = clamp( sensitivity_family * movement, -CAP, +CAP )
    Sensitivities (COMMITTED LAUNCH CALIBRATION; see 3.6):
        LOG_SENSITIVITY       130 BVI points per 1.0 log unit
                              (ln(1.15) = 0.140 -> about 18 points before
                              weighting)
        PCT_POINT_SENSITIVITY   8 BVI points per 1 percentage point
        POSITION_SENSITIVITY   20 BVI points per 1.0 position improved
    CAP = 40 BVI points. This is the v1.2 large-swing cap (old 3.5) reborn.
    A single viral month, campaign spike, or data anomaly cannot push one
    signal past +/-40 points. The raw movement is still displayed; only its
    contribution is capped. A capped signal raises LARGE_SWING (6.1).

3.4 DIMENSION AND COMPOSITE ASSEMBLY
    dimension_contribution = weighted mean of its scorable signals'
      contributions, using the Section 4 intra-dimension weights,
      renormalized over scorable signals (2.4).
    composite_contribution = weighted mean of active dimensions'
      contributions, using Section 2 dimension weights, renormalized over
      active dimensions (2.4).
    BVI = clamp( NEUTRAL + composite_contribution, 0, 100 ), NEUTRAL = 50.
    The linear middle with a soft cap at the tails means assembling first
    then mapping once is equivalent to the intuitive "average the signals"
    read, except at the extremes where the cap protects the score. That
    equivalence is what keeps the number explainable.

3.5 EVERY MONTH IS SCORED (replaces v1.2 3.4 "first month = 55" and v2.0's
    excluded baseline period)
    Under the season-neutral full-cycle mean (2.5), the baseline is a value
    computed from a whole cycle, not a set of months to exclude. Every month
    that carries data is scored as a deviation from that value, including the
    months that fed the baseline. There is no baseline-period exclusion and
    no single-neutral-month rule. A metric whose baseline has not yet locked
    (under a full cycle) still scores, carrying PROVISIONAL_BASELINE (6.1).

3.6 CALIBRATION OF RECORD AND RETUNE TRIGGER
    The Section 3.3 sensitivities were locked by a coherence pass on the
    mapping, reasoning about the score that plausible growth magnitudes
    produce. This is a check on the shape of the mapping, not a fit to any
    client data and not a test against the demo dataset.
    Intended behaviour at the committed values, for a brand moving a broad,
    consistent amount above baseline net of category:
        ~+15% growth  -> BVI near 63   (solid)
        ~+30% growth  -> BVI low 70s   (strong)
        ~+45% or more -> BVI low 80s   (exceptional; approaches the cap)
        flat          -> BVI 50        (neutral, at baseline)
        symmetric decline mirrors the above below 50.
    This places the ordinary operating band at 45-85 with 50 neutral,
    consistent with 1.5, and reserves 85+ for rare, broad strength.
    RETUNE TRIGGER: recompute the sensitivities against observed movement
    once the first cohort of real fashion clients has at least 6 scored
    months each (this arrives with the same pooled data pull that supplies
    the seasonal coefficients, Section 13). Until then these values stand.
    Retuning changes absolute levels only; it does not change score
    direction, ordering, or any logic in Sections 4-6. A retune is a version
    increment with sign-off (per the spec header), not an ad-hoc edit.

SECTION 4: DIMENSION SCORING RULES
-------------------------------------------------------------------

4.1 SEARCH DEMAND (22%)
    Sources: GSC Chart.csv + Queries.csv + Google Trends Export 1.
    Signals and intra weights:
      A. Branded impressions vs baseline   35%   log-ratio (3.1)
      B. Branded clicks vs baseline        25%   log-ratio (3.1)
      C. Brand Trends index vs baseline    25%   log-ratio, DESEASONALIZED
                                                 against category (3.2)
      D. Average position vs baseline      15%   position (3.1)
    Branded metrics methodology (carried unchanged from v1.2):
      Branded clicks = Chart.csv total clicks x branded_share.
      branded_share = branded clicks in Queries.csv / total clicks in
      Queries.csv, computed once and applied to all months. Queries.csv is
      an aggregate with no monthly breakdown; this is a consistent
      approximation.
    Signal D note: average position is read from GSC if the parser exposes
      it. If it does not, D is excluded and A/B/C renormalize. Position is
      never assumed present.
    Missing data:
      GSC missing: A, B, D excluded. Flag DATA_GAP_GSC.
      Trends Export 1 missing: C excluded. Flag DATA_GAP_TRENDS1.
      Both missing: dimension excluded.

4.2 DIGITAL PRESENCE (22%)
    Source: GA4 Traffic Acquisition CSV.
    Signals and intra weights:
      A. Direct sessions vs baseline       30%   log-ratio (3.1)
      B. Direct % of total vs baseline     40%   percentage point (3.1)
      C. Organic sessions vs baseline      30%   log-ratio (3.1)
    Signal B is weighted highest because it is size-agnostic and the
      clearest proxy for brand recall (carried from v1.2).
    Total sessions denominator (carried unchanged): use the GA4 Grand
      Total row as denominator for direct %. Never sum named channels.
    No category comparator exists for Digital, so it is not deseasonalized
      and carries wider uncertainty (6.3). This is a known limit.
    Missing data: GA4 missing, dimension excluded. Flag DATA_GAP_GA4.

4.3 ORGANIC SOCIAL (14%)
    Sources: Instagram (manual entry) + TikTok (manual entry), v1.
    Signals and intra weights:
      A. Engagement rate vs baseline       45%   percentage point (3.1)
      B. Follower growth rate vs baseline  30%   log-ratio (3.1)
      C. Organic reach vs baseline         25%   log-ratio (3.1)
    Signal A highest because reach and follower counts inflate on viral
      one-offs; sustained engagement is harder to game (carried from v1.2).
    Combination (carried): if both platforms present, average the two
      platform values per signal before scoring. If one only, score on it
      and flag SINGLE_PLATFORM_SOCIAL.
    Baseline: with 90 days at onboarding, social is baseline-only until its
      first post-onboarding month (2.5). Flag THIN_BASELINE at minimum
      width. No category comparator, so wider uncertainty (6.3).
    Missing data: no social entered, dimension excluded. Flag
      DATA_GAP_SOCIAL. Partial handled by SINGLE_PLATFORM_SOCIAL.

4.4 COMPETITIVE POSITION (14%)
    Source: Google Trends Export 1.
    Never score on absolute Trends index. Score share, share change, and
      gap change - relative quantities (carried from v1.2).
    Signals and intra weights:
      A. Brand Trends share vs baseline    50%   percentage point (3.1)
      B. Gap to nearest competitor vs base 30%   percentage point (3.1)
      C. Brand Trends index vs baseline    20%   log-ratio (3.1), NOT
                                                 deseasonalized (kept
                                                 distinct from 4.1 C)
    Calculations (carried):
      share = brand_index / sum(all indices in query) x 100
      nearest competitor = highest-index competitor (not the client)
      gap = client_index - nearest_competitor_index (positive = ahead)
    Competitive rank (#1..#4) is display context only; it does not affect
      the score (carried).
    Missing data: Trends Export 1 missing, dimension excluded. Flag
      DATA_GAP_TRENDS1.

4.5 CATEGORY CONTEXT (8%) - REDEFINED (ratify in 0.2)
    Source: Google Trends Export 2 (brand vs category terms).
      SEMrush category volume is display context only, not scored (carried).
    Redefined to measure the brand's standing within its category over
      time, which is distinct from the deseasonalized growth that enters
      Search (4.1 C):
      A. Brand-vs-primary-category gap vs baseline   60%  percentage point
         gap = brand_index - primary_category_index (Export 2)
      B. Brand share of category attention vs baseline 40% percentage point
         share = brand_index / (brand_index + sum(category term indices)) x 100
    Both can move when the brand is flat (for example the category falls,
      so the gap widens and share rises), which is why they carry
      information not already captured by 4.1 C.
    Rising tide (continuous; replaces v1.2 binary flag and 0.90 multiplier,
      ratify 0.3): computed on Export 2 brand vs primary category term.
      RISING_TIDE = TRUE when brand and category move the same direction
      AND the category move is at least 50% of the brand move in magnitude.
      Effect: displayed as a flag (6.1) and used by the badge (6.4) so a
      category-driven gain reads as Watch. No score multiplier.
    Missing data: Trends Export 2 missing, dimension excluded and rising
      tide defaults FALSE. Flag DATA_GAP_TRENDS2.

4.6 SMALL-BASE HANDLING (generalizes v1.2 small-brand rules; see 0.4)
    A near-zero baseline makes a ratio explode and makes volatility
      unmeasurable. Committed launch floors (retune trigger in 0.4):
      Trends index (0-100): FLOOR 5, HARD_MIN 2.
      Counts (impressions, clicks, sessions, reach): FLOOR 100, HARD_MIN 20.
    Rules per ratio metric, by baseline value:
      baseline >= FLOOR:            score as log-ratio (normal).
      HARD_MIN <= baseline < FLOOR: score in point-change mode instead of
                                    ratio, so a small absolute move is not
                                    amplified. Flag SMALL_BASE.
      baseline < HARD_MIN:          not scored; display only. The signal is
                                    excluded and its dimension renormalizes.
                                    Flag SMALL_BASE.
    This subsumes the v1.2 rules (old 4.1 Trends < 10; old 4.5 index 0-2).


-------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 5: SIGNAL COMPOSITION AND COMPARABILITY (carried, re-based)
-------------------------------------------------------------------

5.1 SIGNAL COMPOSITION RECORD
    Every scored month records a sorted fingerprint of the dimension IDs
    that contributed to that month's BVI. Example:
    ["category","competitive","digital","search"].

5.2 MoM DELTA COMPARABILITY
    The displayed MoM delta is shown only when the current and prior scored
    months have identical fingerprints. If they differ, show "-" and flag
    SIGNAL_CHANGE with the added/removed dimensions. Note: the BVI level
    itself is anchored to the same baseline throughout, so the levels are
    comparable; the delta is withheld only to avoid reading a coverage
    change as brand movement.

5.3 A DIMENSION ENTERING FOR THE FIRST TIME
    When a dimension first has data and first contributes in month N (for
    example social, whose data begins after the heavy dimensions): it scores
    normally from that month, carrying PROVISIONAL_BASELINE until it locks a
    full cycle (2.5). The month's MoM delta is "-" and it is flagged
    SIGNAL_CHANGE because the active-dimension set changed.

5.4 A DIMENSION LEAVING (data gap in a previously active month)
    Excluded from that month's BVI (Section 1.3 / 2.4), MoM delta "-",
    flagged DATA_GAP for the source. Prevents a coverage-driven drop from
    reading as brand decline.


-------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 6: FLAGS, UNCERTAINTY, CONFIDENCE, BADGE
-------------------------------------------------------------------

6.1 FLAGS (all displayed, never suppressed; carried and extended)
    DATA_GAP_GSC / _TRENDS1 / _TRENDS2 / _GA4 / _SOCIAL
        A source is missing; the affected dimension(s) excluded.
    SIGNAL_CHANGE
        Active-dimension set differs from prior month; MoM delta withheld.
    RISING_TIDE
        Category moving with the brand; some movement may be category-wide.
    LARGE_SWING (per signal: _IMPRESSIONS, _CLICKS, _DIRECT, _TRENDS, etc.)
        A signal move was large enough to hit the +/-40-point cap (3.3).
        Raw value shown; contribution capped.
    PROVISIONAL_BASELINE (per active dimension)
        A dimension's baseline has not yet locked a full cycle (under 12
        months of that metric). Its baseline is still expanding and is not
        season-neutral yet; interpret with lower weight (2.5). Common for
        social until it accumulates a full year.
    SEASONAL_PENDING
        The seasonal-adjustment layer (0.6, 3.2) is not yet loaded, so scored
        months still carry their own seasonal component. Present on every
        month until the seasonal coefficients are populated (Section 13).
        Clears automatically once SEASONAL_INDEX is loaded.
    SINGLE_PLATFORM_SOCIAL
        Organic Social scored on one platform only (4.3).
    SMALL_BASE (per metric)
        Baseline below FLOOR; metric in point-mode or display-only (4.6).
    INSUFFICIENT_TIER
        Fewer than 2 dimensions active; show "Insufficient data", not a
        number (6.2).
    WIDE_BAND
        Uncertainty band large this month (6.3). Internal; surfaces on the
        click-through detail, not the homepage.

6.2 CONFIDENCE TIER (carried unchanged from v1.2)
    A coverage badge, deterministic, not a score input. It describes how
    many dimensions feed the score:
      Bronze: Search + Digital + Competitive + Category (any subset active).
      Silver: Bronze + Social Listening + Paid Brand Efficiency active.
      Gold:   Silver + Direct Awareness active.
    Note: "confidence tier" is COVERAGE, a separate idea from the
    statistical uncertainty band in 6.3. They are independent: a Gold
    client in a volatile category can carry a wide band; a Bronze client in
    a stable category can carry a tight one. The word "confidence" stays
    attached to the tier in the UI. The band uses a different name
    ("uncertainty" / "signal-to-noise") and is not prominent (6.3).

6.3 UNCERTAINTY BAND (new; secondary layer; not prominent)
    For each signal, volatility = standard deviation of its month-to-month
    movement changes over scored history (log-changes for ratio signals,
    point-changes for point signals), floored (VOL_FLOOR_LOG 0.02,
    VOL_FLOOR_PCT 0.15) so a very stable metric does not read as zero-noise.
    The band on the BVI is these signal volatilities propagated through the
    Section 3 sensitivities and the active weights. It is deterministic
    given the data.
    Placement: the band is NOT on the dashboard homepage. It is available
    when an AD clicks into a month's detail, and it is documented here so
    anyone may interrogate it. Its two working jobs are the badge (6.4) and
    gating whether a small change is worth narrating (7.3). A wide band on a
    given month raises WIDE_BAND.
    Honesty note: because the band is not prominent, the burden of honesty
    about a shaky month falls on the badge and the narrative. When a move is
    inside the band, the badge must not read Improving/Declining (6.4).

6.4 TRAJECTORY BADGE (new; derived from the number's own direction)
    The badge describes which way the BVI itself has moved, so the number
    and the badge can never contradict (a flat number is always Stable).
    Compute the slope of the BVI over the trailing BADGE_WINDOW scored
    months (default 3).
      Improving:  slope over the window exceeds the band.
      Declining:  slope over the window is below the negative band.
      Stable:     movement within the band.
      Watch:      an otherwise Improving month where RISING_TIDE is active
                  (gains may reflect the category, not the brand), OR a
                  positive level that has begun to slope down beyond the
                  band (an eroding lead). "Watch" is the honest read that a
                  level measure alone would miss.
    A brand well above baseline that starts sliding shows a high number and
    a Watch/Declining badge. That combination is coherent and is the single
    most useful signal a brand-health tool can surface.

SECTION 7: NARRATIVE GENERATION (Claude API) (carried, re-based)
-------------------------------------------------------------------

7.1 WHAT CLAUDE RECEIVES
    After all scores are calculated, Claude API is called once per month
    with: final BVI and MoM delta (if comparable), dimension scores, the
    key signals that drove each dimension, the brand-specific-vs-category
    split where relevant, all active flags, client name, competitors,
    category terms, confidence tier. The uncertainty band is provided so
    Claude can hedge a within-band month.

7.2 WHAT CLAUDE PRODUCES
    A 2-3 sentence narrative. Nothing else. Claude does not recalculate,
    override, or question the scores.

7.3 LOCKED NARRATIVE RULES (carried, re-based to baseline language)
    - Lead with the BVI and its primary driver, framed against baseline
      ("X above where tracking began" rather than "up X since last month").
    - Second sentence: the strongest brand-specific signal, stated as
      growth net of category where that applies.
    - Third sentence if warranted: a flag, watch item, or context note.
    - RISING_TIDE active: must say some movement may be category-wide.
    - LARGE_SWING active: must acknowledge the swing.
    - SIGNAL_CHANGE active: must note the score is not directly comparable
      to the prior month.
    - A within-band month (6.3): do not overstate; describe as broadly flat.
    - No em dashes. No hollow openers ("This month", "Overall", "In
      summary", "Looking at"). No hedging when data is clear. No mention of
      weights, methodology, or scoring mechanics. No banned words (7.4).

7.4 BANNED WORDS (carried unchanged)
    delve, intricate, tapestry, pivotal, underscore, landscape, foster,
    testament, enhance, crucial, moreover, realm, beacon, synergy,
    revolutionize, transformative, game-changer, cutting-edge,
    comprehensive, utilize, robust, leverage, streamline, empower,
    seamlessly, holistic, actionable, impactful, elevate, unlock, nuanced,
    curate.

SECTION 9: PLAIN-LANGUAGE TRANSLATION (surface layer; how an AD explains it)
-------------------------------------------------------------------
The engine works in log-growth and points. The AD and CFO never see those.
The fixed mapping in Section 3 lets the same computation be said plainly:

  Headline: "The BVI is 63, up 8 points from your baseline period. On a
  0-100 scale where 50 is exactly where you started, that means brand
  health is meaningfully stronger than at onboarding."

  Category framing (the strongest CFO line, from deseasonalization):
  "Branded search interest is about 15% above your starting point. The
  category rose about 6% over the same stretch, so roughly 9 points of that
  is your brand specifically, not the category tide lifting everyone."

  Trajectory: "The score has been climbing for three straight months, which
  is why it reads Improving rather than a one-month bump."

  Honest hedge on a quiet month: "Movement this month was inside the range
  we would expect from normal fluctuation, so we are calling it broadly
  flat rather than reading into it."

None of these sentences needs a statistics background. All are exact
restatements of the engine output, not simplifications that lose fidelity.

SECTION 10: TWO-LAYER DESIGN OF RECORD
-------------------------------------------------------------------
ENGINE LAYER: season-neutral baseline-relative log-growth and point
  movement, category deseasonalization on the one search signal, seasonal-
  index subtraction on the volume signals (no-op until loaded), per-signal
  cap, single assembly, single map to 0-100, deterministic. Volatility
  computed separately for the band and badge only.
SURFACE LAYER: plain-language restatement (Section 9), the 0-100 headline,
  the badge, and the narrative. The uncertainty band lives one click deep.
MAPPING: the Section 3 constants, fully listed, are the only bridge between
  the layers. Anyone may open this section and the engine file and trace any
  number end to end. That traceability is the point of the design.

SECTION 13: PENDING IRIS DATA - EXACTLY WHAT CHANGES AT v2.2
-------------------------------------------------------------------
Everything in this spec is implementable now. One input is outstanding: a
pooled monthly panel across PDM fashion clients, used to (1) build the
per-month seasonal coefficients for the volume signals and (2) retune the
score sensitivities on real movement. When that data lands, the following
change - and NOTHING ELSE does. No dimension, weight, signal, flag, or
formula in Sections 1-10 is affected.

13.1 POPULATE SEASONAL_INDEX (activates 0.6 / 3.2b).
     Fill the table SEASONAL_INDEX[sub_vertical][calendar_month][family] with
     the typical level of each volume family in each calendar month relative
     to its annual mean, estimated per fashion sub-vertical from the pooled
     panel. Families: search_volume (branded impressions, branded clicks),
     direct_traffic (direct sessions), organic_traffic (organic sessions),
     social_reach (organic reach), social_growth (follower growth rate).
     Effect: SEASONAL_PENDING clears; scored months stop carrying their own
     seasonal component; single-month reads become trustworthy, not just
     full-cycle trajectory.
     Data needed: real-calendar-month panel (YYYY-MM), per client, per volume
     signal, tagged by fashion sub-vertical, with known-anomaly months
     flagged for exclusion. Longer history per client sharpens the estimate;
     more clients compensate for short individual histories.
     Implementation note: confirm the parser's month-key format against
     parse_calendar_month() before loading, and add the real format if it is
     not already handled.

13.2 RETUNE THE SENSITIVITIES (3.6).
     Recompute LOG_SENSITIVITY, PCT_POINT_SENSITIVITY, POSITION_SENSITIVITY
     (and confirm SMALL-BASE floors, 4.6) against the observed distribution
     of baseline-relative movement in the pooled panel, so the 45-85 band and
     the 50-neutral point map onto real fashion-brand behaviour.
     Effect: absolute score levels firm up. Direction, ordering, and all
     logic are unchanged. This is a version increment with sign-off.

13.3 VALIDATE THE CATEGORY OVERLAP (11.2).
     With multi-client data, check the correlation between Category Context
     (4.5) and the Search deseasonalization (3.2a). If high, collapse per the
     0.2 alternatives.

13.4 CONFIRM PROVISIONAL-BASELINE BEHAVIOUR FOR SOCIAL (2.5).
     With real social histories, confirm the expand-then-lock baseline and
     the wider interim band behave as intended over a full social cycle.

Until v2.2, the engine runs on the season-neutral baseline with the seasonal
layer neutral. It is correct and usable in that state; it simply asks readers
to weigh trajectory over a full cycle rather than over-read a single month.
